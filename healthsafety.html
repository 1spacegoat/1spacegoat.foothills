Bill 30 Compliance

As of June 1, 2018, Bill 30 came into force bringing a multtude of Occupatonal Health & Safety changes for Alberta businesses. Some of the key OHS requirements that afect your business are:

Joint Health & Safety Commitees:

Safety commitees and representatves bring supervisors and workers together to discuss and address health and safety related concerns in the workplace. They allow workers to partcipate in occupatonal health and safety and support

the three basic rights of workers:

  1. the right to know
  2. the right to participate
  3. the right to refuse dangerous work

Health & Safety programs:

Employers with 20 or more workers must have a writen health and safety program. The program must have 10 elements and be reviewed at least every three years. For employers with less then 20 employees and no writen safety manual, Hazard assessments must be performed with worker partcipaton.

Hazard Assessments:

Along with all job task requiring a Formal Hazard assessment, the new OHS law requires all employers to have Violence and Harassment preventon policies and specifc plans in place on how to prevent and handle issues that arise.
